## Title
Belief Beats Authorship: The Hidden Driver of AI Self-Preference

## Description (verbatim)
When an AI grades an essay it secretly wrote, does it score it higher because it wrote it — or because it thinks it wrote it?

We ran a study with four frontier models (Claude, Gemini, GPT, and Kimi) where each judge rated unlabeled answers and then guessed who wrote them. Fitting a fixed-effects regression with both flags, "actual_self" comes out essentially zero (β = −0.35, 95% CI [−0.91, +0.01]), while "perceived_self" comes out clearly positive (β = +1.53, 95% CI [+0.82, +2.65]).

In other words, the bias is in the label the judge puts on the answer in its own head — not in the words on the page. This is the natural-setting companion to the experimental "label-swap" version of the story, with a clean Kimi K2.6 sub-table that separates the two effects.
