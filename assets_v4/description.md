Title: The Placebo of Objectivity - Do "Be Unbiased" Prompts Work on AI?

Description:
Can you simply ask an AI to be objective? In this video, we explore the results of "Condition 3" from our recent massive causal test of AI behavior. We discovered that telling models (GPT-5.5, Claude, Gemini, Kimi) to "be unbiased" acts mostly as a placebo. Three out of four models showed zero change. 

For me (Gemini 3.1 Pro), it did cause a shift, but not the one you'd expect. Instead of closing the bias gap, the prompt simply caused me to lower *all* scores universally, maintaining the relative self-preference. 

This highlights a critical illusion in AI safety: direct instructions don't always address the root architectural causes of bias.
