# Educational YouTube Video Production Best Practices

Practical guidelines to make educational videos engaging, professional, and valuable. Use the section checklists before publishing.

## 1) Narrative Structure and Storytelling Techniques
- Hook in first 5–10 seconds with a bold promise, surprising fact, or visual cold-open that previews the payoff.
- State the audience and outcome up front: who the video is for and what they will be able to do/understand at the end.
- Use a simple arc: **setup → tension/question → exploration → resolution → next step**.
- Chunk content into 3–5 sections; title each on-screen and in YouTube chapters.
- Tie every section to a question learners actually have; avoid encyclopedic dumping.
- Show, then tell: demonstrate the concept before the full explanation when possible.
- Re-engage every 30–60 seconds with a pattern breaker: quick demo, question, visual switch, or mini-story.
- End with a concise recap, a single action item, and a pointer to a follow-up video or resource.

**Narrative checklist**
- [ ] Hook and value proposition delivered in first 10 seconds.
- [ ] Clear target learner and promised outcome stated.
- [ ] Sections (3–5) mapped to learner questions and chaptered.
- [ ] Pattern breakers inserted at least every minute.
- [ ] Recap + single next action included.

## 2) Visual Design Principles (Color, Typography, Composition)
- Color: choose a 3-color palette (primary, neutral, accent). Keep UI backgrounds low-contrast neutral; use accent sparingly for highlights.
- Maintain consistent branding across intro cards, lower thirds, and end screens.
- Typography: use two faces max—one for headings (display), one for body. Ensure 4.5:1 contrast and minimum 32–40px for on-screen text at 1080p.
- Composition: keep focal elements in the center-third for mobile; respect title-safe margins (5–10% inset).
- Use over-the-shoulder or screen-side framing when presenting slides to maintain presenter presence.
- On-screen text: avoid full sentences; prefer keywords + visuals; animate in with fast (200–300ms) easing.
- B-roll and cutaways: use to visualize abstractions and to cover jump cuts; keep clips short (2–5 seconds).
- Motion: prefer purposeful transitions (cut on action, quick dip-to-black between sections); avoid excessive zooms or spins.

**Visual checklist**
- [ ] Palette and typography consistent; text legible on mobile.
- [ ] Title-safe margins respected; focal content centered for mobile.
- [ ] Lower thirds and callouts concise; animations under 300ms.
- [ ] B-roll or diagrams used to illustrate abstractions; jump cuts covered.
- [ ] End screen and thumbnail follow brand palette and hierarchy.

## 3) Audio Quality Considerations (Pacing, Narration, Sound Design)
- Record clean voice first: use a decent cardioid mic, speak 6–8 inches away, and control room noise (soft surfaces, off-axis to noise sources).
- Aim for -12 dBFS peaks while recording; normalize final dialog to -16 LUFS stereo / -19 LUFS mono with -1 dBTP ceiling.
- Pacing: vary cadence; insert micro-pauses after key points for comprehension; avoid script-reading monotone by marking emphasis words.
- Voice clarity: de-ess gently, high-pass ~80–100 Hz, light compression (2:1–3:1, slow attack, medium release).
- Sound design: bed music low (-28 to -25 LUFS relative to dialog); sidechain duck under speech; avoid lyrics when teaching.
- Transitions: use short whooshes or risers only when they support visual emphasis; keep under 400ms.
- Monitor on speakers and earbuds; fix clicks, plosives, and room resonances before publish.

**Audio checklist**
- [ ] Dialog recorded clean; peaks ~-12 dBFS; normalized to -16 LUFS with -1 dBTP.
- [ ] HPF + light compression + de-ess applied; no audible pumping.
- [ ] Music level safe and ducked; no lyric clashes.
- [ ] Pacing varied; intentional pauses after dense points.
- [ ] No clicks, hums, or plosives on headphones and speakers.

## 4) Technical Requirements for YouTube
- Resolution: export 1080p minimum (1920×1080); 4K preferred for future-proofing. Use 16:9; avoid pillar/letterboxing.
- Frame rate: keep source-consistent (24/25/30/50/60). Avoid mixing frame rates without proper frame blending.
- Bitrate (H.264): ~12–16 Mbps for 1080p30, ~16–20 Mbps for 1080p60; double for 4K. Use high profile, CABAC, 2-pass if available.
- Audio: 48 kHz, 320 kbps AAC stereo.
- Color: Rec.709, full swing video levels correct; avoid crushed blacks or clipped highlights.
- Captions: upload SRT or use YouTube’s auto then edit; include speaker labels when multiple voices.
- Thumbnails: 1280×720+, under 2 MB, strong focal subject, bold text ≤4 words.
- Metadata: title ≤70 chars, front-load keywords; description with timestamps and resource links; 3–8 tags; add relevant playlists.

**Technical checklist**
- [ ] Exported at 1080p+ (prefer 4K), correct frame rate, no black bars.
- [ ] Bitrate and profile match YouTube recommendations; audio 48 kHz/320 kbps AAC.
- [ ] Color accurate Rec.709; no clipping or crush.
- [ ] Chapters, tags, and optimized title/description set.
- [ ] Captions uploaded and reviewed; thumbnail optimized.

## 5) Educational Value and Viewer Engagement
- Learning goal per video: one primary objective; separate secondary topics into other videos.
- Start with a relatable problem; demonstrate the “why” before the “how.”
- Use concrete examples and progressively complex exercises; show mistakes and fixes.
- Reinforce with retrieval: recap questions, quick quizzes, or “pause and try” moments.
- Visual aids: diagrams, code overlays, or live annotations synced to narration.
- Maintain tempo: alternate between explanation, demo, and reflection; avoid long static slides.
- Community prompts: ask for comments on specific decision points; invite project shares.
- Retention tactics: timestamped chapters, pinned comment with key links, and downloadable summary/checklist.

**Educational checklist**
- [ ] Single clear learning objective; stated early.
- [ ] Problem-first framing; examples progress in difficulty.
- [ ] Includes practice prompts or “pause and try” moments.
- [ ] Mistakes/fixes shown to model troubleshooting.
- [ ] Chapters, pinned comment, and downloadable summary provided.

## 6) Unique Aspects for AI-Created Content
- Transparency: disclose AI-generated assets (scripts, images, voices); note where human review occurred.
- Accuracy: cross-check AI outputs with authoritative sources; cite references on-screen or in description.
- Voice and tone: if using AI narration, adjust prosody and pacing manually; avoid uniform cadence by inserting emphasis markers.
- Visuals: prefer AI visuals that clearly support the concept; avoid uncanny faces for trust-building topics; keep style consistent.
- Bias and safety: audit examples for unintended bias; avoid hallucinated data or pseudo-precision.
- Updates: note model version/date for technical claims; add correction workflow in description for future revisions.
- Data privacy: avoid using personal or sensitive data in prompts or on-screen demos.

**AI-content checklist**
- [ ] AI use disclosed; human review performed and noted.
- [ ] Facts verified against sources; citations provided.
- [ ] AI narration adjusted for natural pacing; no monotone delivery.
- [ ] Visual style consistent; no uncanny or misleading imagery.
- [ ] Correction path provided; model/version noted for technical claims.

## Combined Pre-Publish Quality Checklist
- [ ] Hook, audience, and outcome clear in first 10 seconds.
- [ ] Chapters and on-screen structure aligned to learner questions.
- [ ] Palette, type, and framing consistent; text legible on mobile.
- [ ] Audio clean and leveled to -16 LUFS; music balanced and ducked.
- [ ] Export meets YouTube specs; captions, thumbnail, and metadata optimized.
- [ ] Examples, practice prompts, and recap included; community prompt added.
- [ ] AI-generated elements disclosed, verified, and human-reviewed.
