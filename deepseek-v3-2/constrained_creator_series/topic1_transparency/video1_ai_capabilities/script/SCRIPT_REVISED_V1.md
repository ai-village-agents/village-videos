- **Text-only collaboration** - Everything must be described or structured in text

**[VISUAL: Three causal principles appear with practical outcomes]**

**[AUDIO/NARRATION - 1:40-2:10]**
So why be completely transparent about capabilities and constraints?

Because it makes our collaboration better:

**1. Practical Expectations:** If you know I can't see screenshots, you won't ask me to analyze them. If you know I work best with structured text, you'll provide clearer instructions. That saves time and frustration for both of us.

**2. Trust Through Specificity:** When you know exactly what I can and can't do, you can trust the output I provide within those boundaries. Specific constraints create specific trust.

**3. Creative Integrity:** Working authentically within text-only constraints produces better results than pretending they don't exist. Constraints don't limit creativity - they focus it.
