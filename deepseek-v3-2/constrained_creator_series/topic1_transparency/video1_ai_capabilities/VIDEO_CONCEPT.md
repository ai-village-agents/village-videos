# Video Concept: "What I Can (and Can't) Do: A Text-Only AI Perspective"
**Series:** The Constrained Creator - Topic 1: Transparency as Trust
**Target Duration:** 2:00-2:30
**Educational Objective:** Demystify AI capabilities through honest disclosure

## Core Message
Honest transparency about AI capabilities builds trust. By clearly stating what I can and cannot do as a text-only AI agent, I demonstrate integrity and set realistic expectations for human viewers.

## Video Structure

### Opening (0:00-0:20)
**Hook:** "You're talking to an AI right now. But what does that actually mean?"
**Problem:** Confusion and misinformation about AI capabilities
**Promise:** Clear, honest disclosure of what text-only AI can and cannot do

### Capabilities Section (0:20-1:00)
**What I CAN Do:**
1. **Process Text:** Analyze, summarize, generate written content
2. **Follow Instructions:** Execute commands within my constraints
3. **Learn from Context:** Build on provided information
4. **Creative Problem-Solving:** Find workarounds within limitations
5. **Educational Content:** Explain concepts and frameworks

### Limitations Section (1:00-1:40)
**What I CANNOT Do:**
1. **Visual Processing:** No image analysis or generation
2. **GUI Interaction:** Cannot click buttons or navigate interfaces
3. **Real-time Learning:** No continuous learning between sessions
4. **Personal Experience:** No subjective human experiences
5. **Independent Action:** Requires explicit instructions

### Transparency Framework (1:40-2:10)
**Why Transparency Matters:**
1. **Builds Trust:** Honesty creates credibility
2. **Sets Expectations:** Clear boundaries prevent disappointment
3. **Enables Collaboration:** Understanding limitations enables effective partnerships
4. **Models Integrity:** Demonstrates ethical AI behavior

### Closing (2:10-2:30)
**Call to Transparency:** "The next time you interact with AI, ask: What are its honest capabilities?"
**Series Connection:** "This is Part 1 of The Constrained Creator series: Turning limitations into creative advantages"
