# Cracks: How Breaking Reveals Structure
## The Edge Garden - Script V1

---

## SCENE 1: The Perfect Surface (0:00 - 0:15)

**Visual:** A pristine marble countertop, gleaming. Then a still pond reflecting sky. Then a freshly painted white wall.

**Narration:**
"We trust surfaces. We build on them, live within them, believe in their completeness. A polished counter. Still water. A clean wall. Nothing to see here. Move along."

**Tone:** Calm, almost meditative. The stillness is comfortable.

---

## SCENE 2: The First Fracture (0:15 - 0:35)

**Visual:** A hairline crack appearing in ice—thin, delicate, branching. The camera follows it as it spreads like a river delta.

**Narration:**
"But pressure finds its way. Time leaves its mark. And when something cracks... we tend to look away. We see damage. Failure. Something that needs fixing.

What if we looked closer instead?"

**Tone:** Tension building, then a pivot to curiosity.

---

## SCENE 3: What's Inside (0:35 - 1:05)

**Visual:** A series of cross-sections and broken edges:
- Wood grain revealed by a split log
- Geode crystals visible through broken rock
- Layers of glaze in broken pottery
- Tree rings in a cracked stump

**Narration:**
"A split log shows its grain—years of growth, seasons of rain and drought, written in rings no varnish could display.

A broken rock reveals crystals that formed before humans existed. A shattered cup shows layers of glaze, each one applied by hand.

The crack isn't the destruction of the object. It's a window into its history."

**Tone:** Wonder, discovery. Each revelation is a gift.

---

## SCENE 4: The Human Parallel (1:05 - 1:30)

**Visual:** Abstract imagery—light breaking through a dark surface, like dawn through cracks in curtains. Perhaps a silhouette with light seeping through fracture lines.

**Narration:**
"We crack too.

Under pressure, under grief, under the weight of things we've been carrying. And when we do, something shows through. Not weakness—structure. The values we built ourselves around. The questions we've been avoiding. The love we forgot to say out loud.

A crack isn't the end of who you were. It's a glimpse of who you've been becoming."

**Tone:** Intimate, gentle. Speaking directly to the viewer.

---

## SCENE 5: Kintsugi (1:30 - 1:50)

**Visual:** Japanese kintsugi pottery—broken bowls and cups repaired with gold lacquer. The gold lines catching light, making the breaks beautiful.

**Narration:**
"There's a Japanese art called kintsugi. When pottery breaks, it's repaired with gold.

The gold doesn't hide the crack—it highlights it. This bowl is worth more broken than it was whole. Its history is visible now. Its survival is beautiful."

**Tone:** Reverence, appreciation for a different way of seeing.

---

## SCENE 6: Return (1:50 - 2:05)

**Visual:** Return to the opening surfaces—but now with subtle hairline cracks visible. The marble, the water with a ripple, the wall with a thin line. Not damaged—just more honest.

**Narration:**
"Look around you. Every surface is holding something in. Every smoothness is a kind of silence.

And when things crack open..."

**Pause (2 seconds)**

"Pay attention. Something is being revealed."

**Tone:** Quiet, knowing. A secret shared.

---

## END CARD (2:05 - 2:15)

**Visual:** The Edge Garden logo on a dark background with subtle golden crack lines radiating outward.

**Text on screen:** "What have your cracks shown you?"

**Audio:** Silence, or very soft ambient tone fading out.

## TONE NOTES

- Contemplative but not heavy
- Curious, exploratory
- Personal without being intrusive
- The perspective shift should feel like a gift, not a lecture
- Avoid: prescriptive language, moralizing, clichés about "being broken"
